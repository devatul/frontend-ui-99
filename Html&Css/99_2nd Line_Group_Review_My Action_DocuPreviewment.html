Average Centroid Distance

This represents the average level of deviance from the centroid point, where the centroid point represents the optimal point of classification. The smaller the average deviation, the greater the level of confidence in 99’s classification results.

Maximum Centroid Distance

This represents the document that has been registered furthest from the centroid point. The shorter the distance from the centroid point, the greater the level of confidence in 99’s classification results.

 9.6
Minimum Centroid Distance

This represents the document that has been registered closest to the centroid point. Documents classified closest to the centroid will demonstrate the highest level of confidence that 99’s classification is correct.

 1.6
Centroid Distance Variance

This represent the document variance within the selected cluster. Variance is the expectation of the squared deviation of a random variable from its mean, and it informally measures how far the document are spread out from their cluster’s centroid.
